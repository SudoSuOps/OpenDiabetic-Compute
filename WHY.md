# Why We're Building This

*In Donovan Mackey's words. The founding why of OpenDiabetic & LocalDiabetic.*

---

I've spent thirty years in commercial real estate. Top 1% in the country, a national
platform, billions closed. Best of the best at what I do.

None of it matters to my disease.

I'm a Type 1 diabetic. I've lived the full continuum every single day — the insulin,
the medical bills, the insurance fights, the recovery, the diet, the intake. It is a
24/7 job. You don't get a day off. Ignore it and diabetes will crush you. It will take
the quiet enjoyment out of your life if you let it.

I know its strength and its weakness firsthand, because it's taken from me. I've lost
my right big toe. I've lost others. Fourteen foot surgeries. I just spent fifteen days
in the hospital — four surgeries, two more toes gone, on my driving foot. And I have an
appointment with my podiatrist Monday morning.

Here's the question that started this: **without a swarm of help, how would I even get
there?**

That's the why.

If diabetes can do this to me — a man with every resource, at the top of his field —
imagine what it does to someone alone. Someone who can't afford the shoes. Someone with
no ride to the doctor. Someone staring at a stack of medical bills with nobody in their
corner. It gets lonely. I know.

So we're building the thing I needed. The thing they need.

**OpenDiabetic** is the compute — the hive. The first diabetic compute datacenter. It
earns income, and we recycle the net income straight back into the people.

**LocalDiabetic** is the hand on the shoulder — the healers. It lives in your home, on
your own box. It keeps your records, reminds you, preps you for the doctor, and connects
you to real help.

And the part that matters most: **it costs you nothing.**

You need a ride to and from the doctor? LocalDiabetic has you covered. You need a new
pair of diabetic shoes? We ship them. No charge. No strings. No catch.

> **Give people what they need, not what you have.**

That's the whole philosophy. Not what's convenient for us to give — what *they* need. A
ride. Shoes. Supplies. Recovery. A little peace of mind. Life deserves a give-back. A
helping hand. A solid.

This isn't charity. It's dignity. It's buying back some of the quiet this disease tries
to steal.

And your data never leaves your house. We don't sell it, we don't harvest it, we don't
even see it. The compute and the models come to you; your records stay yours. Every
action leaves a receipt. No black box. We are math and code.

**Where we are, honestly:** the reminders and the appointment prep are real and live
today. The ride network and the shoe shipping are the framework — not the live service
yet. That's what we build next, and it will never be vaporware. Real people are counting
on it.

I'm building this from my own scars. That's why it's real.

— Donovan
