# The Model

*How OpenDiabetic gives free forever without going broke. In Donovan Mackey's words.
Read [WHY.md](WHY.md) first.*

---

At the end of the day, we are **smart money, Defendable, for the people who need it.**

We don't have a warehouse. We don't have supply sitting on a shelf. What we have is
**compute and real-world experience operating it.** That's the edge. Most charities are
buckets with a hole in the bottom — money goes in, a little dribbles out to the need, and
they spend the rest of their life begging to fill the bucket again.

We don't fill a bucket. We dig a well.

## Charity burns the seed. We plant it.

A normal $100 donation, on its best day, gets maybe 10–20% to the actual need. The other
80–90% feeds the machine, and the principal is gone. $100 in, $100 out, back to begging.

Here's what we do with that same $100:

- We buy a GPU and put it on the marketplace. A dev, a researcher, a business rents it.
- They pay us, say, $10 a month. That's $120 a year — **and we never burned the $100.**
- After lean overhead, that's real money to buy the shoes and ship them, free.
- **And the GPU is still working.** It keeps printing for the next five years.
- When it gets old, we sell it and buy the next generation.

We don't burn donations. We **10x them** and give the people what they need. The seed
becomes a tree, and the tree keeps dropping fruit.

## Three ways to give — every one of them works

- **Cash** — we buy what the moment needs. An income GPU for the endowment, or a Synology
  NAS for Mary.
- **Compute** — donate a 5090 and it goes straight on the market. A real worker, earning,
  hosting models. It feeds the give-engine.
- **A device** — donate an Apple Watch, we ship it to Jane for her reminders. Donate an
  iPhone, John needed one. Donate a NAS, it's Mary's vault by Friday.

I just donated **$1M in compute to OpenDiabetic** — a fleet of RTX 6000s. Those rigs will
fine-tune our models, host the models LocalDiabetic runs, and earn income from research
departments that need real compute. Real income, real value, feeding an overhead that's
**lean and mean — not a bloated rug pull.** When they've run their years, we recycle them
and buy the next gen.

## It doesn't just give. It creates jobs.

This is the part people miss. Every give creates work, and the work spreads.

We ship Mary, 75 years old, the NAS she needs. Mary has no clue how to set it up. So we
send **Joe — a LocalDiabetic volunteer — and we pay him $50 for his time.** Joe wants to
participate. Joe's dad is a diabetic too, so we ship his dad a cookbook and set him up
with a diet. And Joe's brother? He owns a law firm that needs a light compute eval for
operations — so we ship him an **edge pack that plugs into the OpenDiabetic network, for a
fee.** We give him what he needs, light-lift compute, and **it compounds.**

One NAS for Mary turned into a paid job, two more people helped, and a paying customer.

## And you can watch every dollar of it

The reason "we give it all back" usually smells like a rug pull is nobody can see the
money. You take their word for it. We don't do trust-me-bro. **We are math and code.**

Every donation, every rental dollar, every give, every job — receipted, hash-chained,
visible. Follow your own dollar: *this $100 → that GPU → earned $617 → bought Mary's NAS
and paid Joe.* No charity on earth lets you trace that. We do. The anti-rug-pull isn't a
promise — it's a ledger.

## Where we are, honestly

The compute, the models, the vault, the reminders, the receipts — real and live. The

framework we're building now.** It will never be vaporware. Real people are counting on it.

That's really it. We build compute. We create jobs. We feed the machine. We give people
what they need.
